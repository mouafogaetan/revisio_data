Section A : Reading Comprehension (10 marks)

Document A : extracted from "Youth Voices on Social Change" (adapted)

In many communities across Cameroon, harmful traditional practices such as female genital mutilation (FGM) still persist, despite laws prohibiting them. Perpetrators are often family members who believe these customs are necessary for cultural acceptance. Teenagers and young adults are becoming increasingly aware of these social ills. Nabila, 18, from the Far North region, says: “My own aunt wanted to take me to a traditional practitioner. I refused and reported the case to a local NGO. That’s when I understood that ignorance perpetuates abuse.”

Beyond FGM, other issues like lack of official documents hinder many young people’s access to education and jobs. Obtaining a birth certificate or a national ID card remains a challenge in rural areas. Activists argue that civic responsibility includes knowing one's rights and helping others navigate administrative procedures. “We started a youth group to explain how to get a birth certificate. It is a basic right,” explains Marc, a community leader in Yaoundé.

Furthermore, the relevance of the educational system is questioned. Many students feel that the curriculum does not always prepare them for real-life challenges. Yet, education remains a powerful tool to fight against social ills and foster responsible citizens.

[Source: adapted from Cameroon Youth Chronicles, 2026]

1. (2 marks) – According to the text, who are the perpetrators of female genital mutilation (FGM) in the communities described? Quote one element from the text.

2. (2 marks) – What administrative problem do many young people face in rural areas, and how do activists like Marc propose to solve it?

3. (3 marks) – Find in the text words or expressions that mean the same as:

  • a) harmful customs / traditions (paragraph 1) : ....................
  • b) people who carry out a harmful action (paragraph 1) : ....................
  • c) lack of knowledge (paragraph 1) : ....................

4. (3 marks) – Based on the document, explain the sentence: “Education remains a powerful tool to fight against social ills.” Provide two arguments from your own knowledge.

Section C : Written Production (4 marks)

Choose only ONE of the following two topics. Write between 120 and 180 words. Pay attention to grammar, coherence, and vocabulary.

✨ Topic 1 : Civic Engagement & Administrative procedures

You are a senior prefect in your school. Many students from rural areas struggle to obtain their birth certificates or national ID cards. Write a speech (for the school assembly) to raise awareness about the importance of official documents and explain how to start the procedure. Use modal verbs for polite requests and advice (can, could, should).

💼 Topic 2 : The World of Work – Job Interview Simulation

You are applying for a part-time job as a sales assistant in a local bookstore. Write a cover letter or a short dialogue where you introduce yourself, mention your qualities (honest, hardworking, good communication skills), and explain why you are interested in the job. Use the present perfect tense (e.g., “I have already volunteered in a library...”).
