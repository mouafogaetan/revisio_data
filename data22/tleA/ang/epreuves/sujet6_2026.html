Document The weight of a choice

Adapted from an interview with Amina, a young Cameroonian journalist.

“Growing up in Douala, everyone expected me to become a doctor or an engineer. My father used to say, ‘A diploma is your passport to a stable life.’ But I had a secret dream: telling stories, giving a voice to those who are silenced. At seventeen, I had to make a choice that would determine my whole future.

I remember the night I told my parents I wanted to study journalism. My mother remained silent for a long minute, then whispered: ‘And what about security? What about money?’ For weeks, our home felt like a battlefield. However, I could not ignore my passion. I decided to apply for a bachelor’s degree in communication. I might face financial difficulties, but I might also find real fulfillment.

Today, at twenty-five, I work for a local newspaper. I don’t earn as much as a doctor, but I wake up every morning excited. My latest investigation was about girls who dropped out of school because of early marriage. By telling their stories, I feel useful. Emigrating was never my goal; I want to build my own identity right here, and help my society change from within.”

PART 1 Reading Comprehension (8 points)

1. Answer the following questions based on the text. (4 pts)

  • What was Amina’s family expecting her to become? (1 pt)
  • According to the text, why did Amina’s mother worry about journalism? (1 pt)
  • How does Amina feel about her current job today? Justify with a short quote. (1 pt)
  • What social issue does Amina mention investigating? (1 pt)

2. True or False? Justify with a sentence from the text. (2 pts)

  • Amina’s parents immediately supported her choice. ________
  • She has always wanted to emigrate to find better opportunities. ________

3. Find words or expressions in the text that mean the same as: (2 pts)

  • « calm and without any noise » (paragraph 2) : ______________
  • « happiness and satisfaction » (paragraph 3) : ______________

PART 2 Grammar in context (4 points)

A. Modals of speculation (may, might, could). (2 pts)

Rewrite the sentences using a modal of speculation to show possibility.

  1. Perhaps Amina will write a book about her experience one day.
    ➜ Amina write a book about her experience.
  2. It is possible that her investigations inspire other young girls.
    ➜ Her investigations inspire other young girls.

B. Linking words (because, therefore, however, although). (2 pts)

Fill in the blanks with the correct linking word.

  1. ________ journalism does not always pay well, Amina finds deep satisfaction in her job.
  2. Her parents were afraid for her future; ________, they finally accepted her decision.

PART 3 Vocabulary building (4 points)

1. Match the words with their definitions. (2 pts)

1. emigrate ➜ a) a feeling of belonging to a group or culture
2. identity ➜ b) to leave your own country to live in another
3. awareness ➜ c) knowledge or understanding of a particular subject
4. early marriage ➜ d) a social issue where children are forced to wed

Write your answers: 1-__ , 2-__ , 3-__ , 4-__

2. Complete the sentences with the right word. (2 pts)

(abroad / opportunities / consequences / prevention)

  • Many young graduates look for better job __________ in big cities.
  • The government launched a campaign for the __________ of school violence.

PART 4 Written expression (4 points)

Choose ONE of the following topics. Write between 120 and 150 words.

Option A : Speech

You are the senior prefect of your school. Drug consumption is increasing among students. Write a speech to address the school assembly: give causes, consequences, and solutions. Use at least two linking words (because, therefore, however, although).

Option B : Letter of motivation

Write a letter to your parents to explain a personal choice (e.g., choosing a specific career path, staying in your hometown instead of emigrating, or taking a gap year). Persuade them of your decision using arguments based on your identity and future plans. Use will and be going to.
