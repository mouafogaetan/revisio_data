📖 Partie 1 : Compréhension écrite (08 points)

Document : "Volunteer to Succeed"

Many young jobseekers focus only on paid positions, ignoring the power of volunteering. Yet, community engagement builds essential skills that classrooms rarely teach. Take Sarah, a 22-year-old graduate who spent six months helping a local NGO manage social media campaigns. "I had never written a professional email before," she says. "But coordinating volunteers taught me teamwork, time management, and even basic budgeting." When she later applied for a junior project manager role, the interviewer was impressed by her concrete examples of problem-solving. "My CV was not just a list of degrees; it showed impact."

However, not everyone recognises this value. Some employers still prioritise formal experience. But a growing number of companies now ask: "Have you ever volunteered?" According to a recent survey, 78% of hiring managers believe volunteering counts as valid work experience. In a competitive job market, candidates who combine academic qualifications with civic commitment stand out. As one HR director puts it: "We can teach technical skills, but we cannot teach empathy and initiative."

1. (01 pt) – D'après le texte, quelle est l'erreur fréquente des jeunes chercheurs d'emploi ?

2. (01,5 pt) – Relevez deux compétences que Sarah a acquises grâce au volontariat.

3. (01 pt) – Que signifie l'expression : "My CV was not just a list of degrees; it showed impact." ?

4. (01,5 pt) – Selon l'enquête mentionnée, pourquoi le bénévolat est-il un atout ?

✍️ Partie 2 : Maîtrise de la langue (06 points)

A Grammaire – Parcours professionnel

✔️ Present perfect / Modals of advice & obligation

6. (02 pts) Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du verbe entre parenthèses ou le modal adéquat (should / have to / mustn't) :

  1. She (apply) ______ for three internships since January.
  2. You ______ (respect) the dress code during a job interview; it’s mandatory.
  3. They are happy because they (find) ______ a promising job advertisement.
  4. To succeed, you ______ (not / neglect) networking opportunities.

B Expression de but & vocabulaire professionnel

📝 Partie 3 : Production écrite (06 points)

📌 Choix du sujet :

Traitez un seul des deux sujets suivants. La clarté, la cohérence et la qualité linguistique seront évaluées (environ 150 mots).

Sujet 1 Lettre de motivation

Vous avez vu l’annonce suivante : "Junior Digital Assistant – Youth Empowerment NGO. Requirements: good communication skills, ability to work in a team, some experience in social media." Rédigez une lettre de candidature structurée (vous pouvez inventer vos coordonnées et expériences). Utilisez le vocabulaire de la leçon 5 (position, requirements, apply, cover letter).

Sujet 2 Article pour le journal du lycée

Vous avez participé à une action de bénévolat (nettoyage, soutien scolaire, etc.). Rédigez un court article expliquant pourquoi le volontariat est précieux pour l’avenir professionnel des jeunes. Appuyez-vous sur les arguments de la leçon 6 (skills, community, benefits, experience) et utilisez "to + infinitive" pour exprimer le but.
