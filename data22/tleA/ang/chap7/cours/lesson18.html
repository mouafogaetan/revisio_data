Section A

Reading Comprehension (20 marks)

📖 Read the text below carefully, then answer the questions.

The Hidden Scars: Understanding Social Ills in Our Communities

In many societies, certain harmful practices persist despite laws and awareness campaigns. Female genital mutilation (FGM), for example, remains a violation of human rights that affects millions of girls worldwide. Perpetrators are often family members who believe that tradition justifies the act. Social workers and activists argue that education is the most powerful weapon to break this cycle. "Communities that empower girls through education witness a dramatic drop in such abuses," says Sarah Nkosi, a Cameroonian child protection advocate.

Beyond FGM, other social ills like child labour and domestic violence also tear the fabric of communities. A responsible citizen has a duty to report any form of abuse. The Cameroonian Penal Code clearly condemns these acts, but enforcement remains a challenge in remote areas. Civic responsibility is not just about obeying laws; it is about building a culture where every individual’s dignity is protected.

Grassroots organisations have started community dialogues where perpetrators and victims can speak — a bold step towards healing. As one elder put it, “A society that ignores its social ills plants seeds of its own destruction.” It is the duty of every citizen, young or old, to speak up and act. The future depends on a generation that refuses to remain silent.

Adapted from various awareness campaigns, Cameroon 2025.

1. According to the text, why does female genital mutilation (FGM) continue in some communities? (2 marks)

2. Find in the text a synonym for “illegal acts” or “wrongdoings”. (1 mark)

3. What does the phrase “perpetrators are often family members” imply about the complexity of fighting FGM? (2 marks)

4. Grammar in context: Identify the relative pronoun in this sentence from the text: “Communities that empower girls through education witness a dramatic drop.” Then explain what it refers to. (2 marks)

5. In your own words, explain the civic responsibility described in the last paragraph. (3 marks)
