🌍 Environmental Awareness

❓ VOCABULARY | Environmental threats
1. Which word best completes the sentence?
"The ________ of toxic chemicals into the river has contaminated the water supply."
  • A. recycle
  • B. disposal
  • C. preservation
  • D. windbreak

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : B. disposal

    🔹 "Disposal" means getting rid of something. Toxic waste disposal is a major environmental issue (cf. lesson 9: waste management).

    📖 GRAMMAR | Causative "have/get"
    2. Choose the correct structure to express:
    "We should pay someone to collect our garbage regularly."
  • A. We should have our garbage collected regularly.
  • B. We should get our garbage collect regularly.
  • C. We should have collected our garbage regularly.
  • D. We should do our garbage collected.

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : A. We should have our garbage collected regularly.

    🔹 Causative: have + object + past participle → have our garbage collected. (Lesson 9: The Problem of Waste)

    📖 READING COMPREHENSION | Waste crisis
    📄 Text: In many urban areas of Cameroon, improper rubbish disposal leads to blocked drainage systems and health risks. Toxic waste dumped near residential zones contaminates soil and groundwater. Environmental experts suggest that local authorities should have waste treatment facilities built to reduce pollution. Citizens also play a key role by sorting their garbage and reducing plastic use.
    3. According to the text, what is one consequence of improper waste disposal?
  • A. Increase in recycling rates
  • B. Contamination of soil and water
  • C. More job opportunities
  • D. Better air quality

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : B. Contamination of soil and water

    🔹 The text explicitly states: "Toxic waste ... contaminates soil and groundwater."

    📖 GRAMMAR | Gerund as subject
    4. Which sentence correctly uses a gerund as the subject?
  • A. To recycle is important for the planet.
  • B. Recycling helps preserve natural resources.
  • C. People recycle to reduce waste.
  • D. Recycled materials are useful.

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : B. Recycling helps preserve natural resources.

    🔹 "Recycling" (verb+ing) functions as the subject of the sentence. (Lesson 10: Promoting Recycling)

    🌱 VOCABULARY | The 3 R's
    5. Match the concept:
    "Using an old glass jar as a storage container instead of throwing it away" is an example of ________.
  • A. Reducing
  • B. Recycling
  • C. Reusing
  • D. Contaminating

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : C. Reusing

    🔹 Reusing means using an item again without processing. The 3 R's: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle (Lesson 10 vocabulary).

    ⛰️ GRAMMAR | Present continuous for ongoing processes
    6. Complete the sentence with the correct verb form:
    "Deforestation continues, and as a result, the topsoil ________ (erode) at an alarming rate."
  • A. is eroding
  • B. erodes
  • C. has eroded
  • D. was eroding

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : A. is eroding

    🔹 Present continuous (is eroding) describes an ongoing process happening now. (Lesson 11: Soil Erosion and Conservation).

    🎧 LISTENING / ECOLOGICAL INITIATIVE
    🎤 Script (teacher reads or students imagine):
    "In the city of Douala, a new community project encourages residents to separate organic waste from plastics. Volunteers collect plastic bottles and turn them into paving stones. The mayor declared: 'Every week, we transform more than 500 kg of plastic into useful materials. This reduces waste and creates jobs.'"
    7. What is the main goal of the initiative described?
  • A. To burn plastic for energy
  • B. To transform plastic waste into paving stones
  • C. To ban all plastic bags
  • D. To export waste to other countries

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : B. To transform plastic waste into paving stones

    🔹 The script says: "collect plastic bottles and turn them into paving stones". This aligns with lesson 10 (recycling / ecological initiative).

    🌾 SOIL EROSION | Understanding the process
    8. Which of the following is an effective solution to prevent soil erosion?
  • A. Removing all trees from slopes
  • B. Planting windbreaks and cover crops
  • C. Increasing the use of chemical fertilizers
  • D. Allowing livestock to overgraze

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : B. Planting windbreaks and cover crops

    🔹 Windbreaks and vegetation hold the soil together and reduce wind/water erosion (Lesson 11: vocabulary "windbreaks, conservation").

    ⚡ TRUE / FALSE | Causative & environment
    9. Statement: "The sentence 'We got a specialist to check the air quality' has the same meaning as 'We had the air quality checked by a specialist'."
  • A. True
  • B. False

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : A. True

    🔹 Both express causative meaning: "got + someone + to infinitive" vs "had + something + past participle" can be similar in certain contexts. The first means we arranged for a person, the second focuses on the action, but both imply we caused it to happen.

    🌍 VOCABULARY | Conservation terms
    10. In the context of soil conservation, "topsoil" refers to:
  • A. The deepest layer of rocks
  • B. The upper, fertile layer of soil
  • C. Artificial fertilizer
  • D. A type of windbreak

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : B. The upper, fertile layer of soil

    🔹 Topsoil is crucial for farming and is vulnerable to erosion (Lesson 11: "topsoil, conservation").

    ✍️ GRAMMAR | Gerund after preposition
    11. Complete: "Many environmentalists insist _______ (reduce) single-use plastics."
  • A. to reduce
  • B. on reducing
  • C. for reduce
  • D. reduce

    • ✅ Réponse correcte : B. on reducing

    🔹 After a preposition (on), we use the gerund (-ing form). "Insist on + gerund" is a common structure.

    📝 WRITING TASK (Short answer)
    12. Based on the chapter "The Problem of Waste" and "Promoting Recycling", propose one simple action that a student can take at school to reduce waste. (Explain in one sentence)
  • → Write your answer mentally before revealing the sample answer.

    • ✅ Exemple de réponse : Students can bring reusable water bottles and lunch containers instead of using disposable plastic bags and cups, which reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills.

    🔹 Other valid answers: sorting waste in classroom bins, starting a paper recycling campaign, avoiding single-use plastics.
