📝 ÉVALUATION SÉQUENTIELLE N°2

The World of Work

Terminale A – Anglais – Durée : 2 heures
📌 Compétences : Comprendre le monde du travail, postuler à un emploi, valoriser le volontariat. ⭐ Thèmes : Job Interview, Application, Volunteering

📖 Part 1: Reading Comprehension (8 pts)

Read the text carefully and answer the questions.

📄 DOCUMENT
🌟 VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITY: GREEN CAMEROUN INITIATIVE 🌟

Are you passionate about the environment? Do you want to make a difference in your community?

Position: Community Recycling Ambassador
Mission: Educate local residents about waste sorting and recycling. Organize weekly clean-up campaigns.
Requirements: Good communication skills, sense of responsibility, available on Saturdays.
Benefits: Gain valuable work experience, develop leadership skills, receive an official certificate.

"Volunteering is the best way to build your CV while helping others."
1. What is the main mission of the Community Recycling Ambassador? (2 pts)

✅ Élément de réponse The main mission is to educate local residents about waste sorting and recycling, and to organize weekly clean-up campaigns.

2. According to the text, what are two benefits of volunteering? (2 pts)

✅ Élément de réponse Two benefits are: gaining valuable work experience and developing leadership skills. (Also accepted: receiving an official certificate).

3. Find in the text a word that means "the quality of being reliable and trustworthy." (2 pts)

✅ Élément de réponse The word is "responsibility" (in the sentence: "sense of responsibility").

4. Why does the text say volunteering is good for your CV? (2 pts)

✅ Élément de réponse Because it allows you to gain work experience and skills (leadership, communication), which are valuable for future job applications.

📖 Part 2: Vocabulary (6 pts)

📝 EXERCISE 1: Match the word with its definition
  • 1. CV (Curriculum Vitae)     A. A formal meeting where you are asked questions to see if you are suitable for a job
  • 2. Qualifications     B. A document showing your work history and education
  • 3. Job Interview     C. The money you receive regularly for doing a job
  • 4. Salary     D. Official records proving you have completed a course or have specific skills

    • ✅ Réponses 1-B, 2-D, 3-A, 4-C

    📝 EXERCISE 2: Fill in the blanks
    Complete the sentences with: volunteer • experience • skills • apply
  • a) I want to __________ for the position of sales assistant.
  • b) She has excellent leadership __________ from her time as class president.
  • c) You don't get paid as a __________, but you learn a lot.
  • d) His three years of teaching gave him valuable __________.

    • ✅ Réponses a) apply, b) skills, c) volunteer, d) experience

    📖 Part 3: Grammar (6 pts)

    🔍 EXERCISE 1: Present Perfect Tense
    Complete the sentences using the present perfect form of the verbs in brackets.
  • a) She __________ (apply) for three different jobs this week.
  • b) I __________ (never / attend) a job interview before.
  • c) They __________ (gain) a lot of experience since they started volunteering.

    • ✅ Réponses a) has applied, b) have never attended, c) have gained

    🔍 EXERCISE 2: Modals (Should / Have to)
    Choose the correct modal.
  • a) You __________ (should / have to) arrive on time for the interview. It's a rule.
  • b) You __________ (should / have to) prepare some questions to ask the employer. It's a good idea.

    • ✅ Réponses a) have to (obligation), b) should (advice)

    🔍 EXERCISE 3: Expressing Purpose
    Join the sentences using "to + infinitive" or "in order to".
  • Example: I study English. I want to get a better job. → I study English to get a better job.
  • a) He volunteers at the hospital. He wants to become a doctor.
  • b) She is saving money. She plans to buy professional clothes.

    • ✅ Réponses a) He volunteers at the hospital to become a doctor. / He volunteers in order to become a doctor.
    b) She is saving money to buy professional clothes. / She is saving money in order to buy professional clothes.

    ✍️ Part 4: Writing (10 pts)

    ✉️ TASK: Cover Letter
    Situation: You are applying for a part-time job as a "Sales Assistant" in a local bookshop.

    Write a short cover letter (80-100 words) including:
    • Your motivation for the job
    • Your qualities/skills (e.g., responsible, punctual, good communication)
    • Your availability

    (Remember to use the grammar points: Present Perfect, Should/Have to, or Purpose expressions if possible.)

    ✅ Exemple de lettre

    Dear Manager,

    I am writing to apply for the Sales Assistant position at your bookshop. I have always loved reading, and I think this job is perfect for me.

    I am a responsible and punctual student. I have already helped organize the library at my school, so I have experience sorting books. I am available every afternoon and on Saturdays.

    I believe I am the right person for this job. I look forward to your reply.

    Sincerely,
    [Your Name]

    🎤 Part 5: Role-play (Bonus / Oral Practice)

    💼 SIMULATION: Job Interview
    Instructions: In pairs, simulate a job interview. One student is the interviewer, the other is the candidate. Use the vocabulary and grammar from the sequence.

    Example questions for the interviewer:
    • "Tell me about yourself."
    • "Why do you want this job?"
    • "Have you had any previous experience?" (Present Perfect)
    • "What are your strengths?"
    Tips for the candidate: Use "I have..." (Present Perfect) to talk about your experiences. Use "should" to give advice or "have to" to talk about obligations.

    ✅ Phrases utiles pour l'oral
    - Introducing yourself: "I am a student at Terminale A. I have always been interested in..."
    - Talking about experience: "I have already volunteered for..." / "I have never worked before, but I am a fast learner."
    - Talking about qualities: "I am hardworking, punctual, and I have good communication skills."
    - Asking politely: "Could you tell me more about the salary?" / "May I ask when I will receive an answer?"
