📖 PART ONE : READING COMPREHENSION (12 points)
📌 Text: "The Weight of a Passport"

"For many young graduates in Cameroon, the idea of travelling abroad is no longer just a dream — it's a calculation. Kareen, 19, holds a Bacc C and scrolls through LinkedIn every evening. 'Here, even with a degree, you wait years for a job that pays fairly. I love my country, but I might try my luck in Canada if things don't change,' she says. Her friend Yannick has a different view: 'Emigration isn't a miracle solution. I could go, but who will help our communities if everyone leaves? Home is where we build, not just where we are born.' Between hope and identity, a whole generation questions what 'future' truly means."

— Adapted from "Youth Voices, Cameroon" (2025)

1. According to the text, what is Kareen’s main motivation for considering emigration? (2 pts)
  • A) Lack of higher education opportunities
  • B) Long waiting periods and low salaries despite her degree
  • C) Family pressure to move abroad
  • D) She hates her country’s culture
    • ✅ Correct: B. "even with a degree, you wait years for a job that pays fairly" → job scarcity & low wages.
    2. How does Yannick’s perspective differ from Kareen’s? (2 pts)
  • A) He thinks emigration is the only solution
  • B) He believes leaving might not solve everything and emphasizes community responsibility
  • C) He wants to emigrate immediately
  • D) He doesn't care about jobs
    • ✅ Correct: B. Yannick says "Emigration isn't a miracle solution" and asks "who will help our communities?" → collective identity.
    3. Find in the text a phrase that shows possibility (modal of speculation). (2 pts)
  • A) "I love my country"
  • B) "I might try my luck in Canada"
  • C) "It's a calculation"
  • D) "Home is where we build"
    • ✅ Correct: B — "might" expresses possibility/speculation (modal).
    4. VOCABULARY (2 pts) : The word "emigration" in the text most nearly means:
  • A) Returning to one's homeland
  • B) Leaving one's country to live permanently elsewhere
  • C) Travelling for holidays
  • D) Studying abroad temporarily
    • ✅ Correct: B. Emigration = leaving your own country to settle in another.
    5. GRAMMAR (Reported speech) : Kareen said, "I might try my luck in Canada if things don’t change." Rewrite in reported speech: (4 pts)
    📝 Kareen said that she might try her luck in Canada if things didn't change. (backshift of tenses & pronouns)
    ✍️ PART TWO : LANGUAGE IN USE (10 points)
    6. Choose the correct future form: "After graduating, I _______ for a master's degree in public health." (intention) (2 pts)
  • A) will apply
  • B) am going to apply
  • C) apply
  • D) Both A and B are possible but 'be going to' shows strong intention
    • ✅ D is the best: both 'will' and 'going to' express intention, but 'am going to' emphasizes a planned intention. Acceptable B/D context.
    7. Complete with a modal of speculation (may / might / could) : "Yannick ________ decide to stay in Cameroon to help his community; we don’t know yet." (2 pts)
    ✅ Correct: might / may / could (any modal expressing possibility). Example: "Yannick might decide to stay..."
    8. Match the linking word with its function: (2 pts)
  • 1. "therefore" →   
  • 2. "although" →   
    • ✅ 1. therefore → consequence ; 2. although → concession/contrast.
    9. Choose the correct word to complete the sentence about school issues: "The senior prefect delivered a strong speech _______ the rise of violence in the schoolyard." (2 pts)
  • A) because
  • B) despite
  • C) concerning / about
  • D) however
    • ✅ C: "concerning" or "about" — "a speech concerning the rise of violence" is appropriate.
    10. (Vocabulary: social issues) "Drug _____________ is one of the main challenges that affects students' concentration." Fill the blank: (2 pts)
    ✅ Possible answers: consumption / abuse / use. (From programme: "drug consumption")
