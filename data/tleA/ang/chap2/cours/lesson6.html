📖 Lesson 6: The Value of Volunteering

Terminale A (Literary Stream) · Module 1: The Individual and Society · Chapter 2: The World of Work

📌 Key Concepts & Vocabulary

🌟 What is Volunteering?

Volunteering means freely giving your time and skills to help a cause, a community, or an organization without expecting financial payment. It builds solidarity, strengthens communities, and develops personal skills.

  • Core benefits : personal growth, professional experience, social connections, and community development.
  • Key vocabulary : volunteer, community, non-profit organization, sense of purpose, empathy, leadership, teamwork.
  • Common fields : education, health, environment, youth mentorship, disaster relief.

📚 Grammar: Expressing Purpose

Use to + infinitive or in order to + infinitive to explain why someone does something. This structure answers the question “Why?” and shows intention.

  • Subject + verb + to + base verb → "She volunteers to gain experience."
  • Subject + verb + in order to + base verb (more formal) → "They join projects in order to improve their community."
  • Negative purpose : use so as not to or in order not to. → "He arrived early so as not to miss the meeting."

💼 Volunteering & Employability

In the world of work, volunteering is a powerful asset for your CV. It shows initiative, responsibility, and civic engagement. Many employers value volunteer experience as much as formal employment.

  • Transferable skills : communication, problem-solving, project management, adaptability.
  • Examples on CV : "Organized a community recycling campaign" or "Tutored 20 high school students after school."
  • Community impact : reduces social inequality, strengthens local networks, promotes active citizenship.

💡 Examples & Application

📖 Example 1: Reading + Purpose Clauses

Text excerpt: “Many young people in Douala volunteer at local health centres. They spend their weekends organizing awareness campaigns. They do this to inform families about malaria prevention. Some also join environmental clubs in order to keep their neighbourhoods clean.”
✅ Analysis: The phrases "to inform families" and "in order to keep their neighbourhoods clean" express purpose.
➡️ Question: Why do young people volunteer at health centres?
Answer: They volunteer to inform families about malaria prevention and in order to raise health awareness.
💬 Tip: To identify purpose, ask “Why does the person do the action?” If the answer begins with “to...” you have a purpose clause.

✍️ Example 2: Cover Letter & Volunteering Motivation

Situation: You are applying for a summer internship. You have volunteered for two years with “Education for All” where you taught English to underprivileged children. Write one sentence explaining why you volunteered.
✔️ Model sentence (using purpose): “I volunteered with Education for All to develop my teaching skills and in order to make education more accessible to vulnerable children.”
✔️ Alternative: “Volunteering allowed me to connect with my community to foster equal opportunities in education.”
🎯 Exam tip: In composition writing (job application or persuasive essay), use purpose structures to show clear motivation. It makes your argument stronger and more coherent.

🎤 Example 3: Speaking – Job Interview Question

Interviewer: “Why did you decide to spend your free time volunteering at the community library?”
Candidate (you): Provide an answer using to / in order to.
Sample answer: “I decided to volunteer at the library to improve my organisational skills and in order to help young students discover the joy of reading. Besides, I believe that giving back to the community is essential for social harmony.”
💡 Remember: after “to” or “in order to”, always use the base form of the verb (no -ing, no past). Example: “to help” ✔️ not “to helping”.

⚠️ Common Mistakes & Pitfalls

  • Confusing purpose with reason: “Because” introduces a reason (cause), while “to” expresses intention.
    ❌ “I volunteer because to get experience.” → ✅ “I volunteer to get experience.”
  • Forgetting the base verb after “to”: ❌ “She volunteered to helping others.” → ✅ “She volunteered to help others.”
  • Overusing “in order to” in informal contexts: Both are correct, but “to” is more natural in everyday speech. Use “in order to” in formal writing or to add emphasis.
  • Not linking volunteering to exam skills: In composition writing (letters, speeches, articles), always illustrate your points with concrete volunteering examples. Examiners value civic awareness.
🚫 Avoid verb form mistakes: purpose = infinitive!

🔁 Quick Glance: Vocabulary + Purpose Structures

📝 Must-Know Expressions (Terminale A exam style)

  • to engage in community service → participer au service communautaire
  • to raise awareness about → sensibiliser à
  • to foster social cohesion → favoriser la cohésion sociale
  • to make a difference → faire la différence
  • to contribute to society → contribuer à la société

🔹 Grammar recap formula:
[action] + to + [verb] → purpose (informal / general)
[action] + in order to + [verb] → purpose (formal / written)
[action] + so as not to + [verb] → negative purpose

📍 Exam connection: In composition writing (e.g., "Write a speech as a senior prefect about fighting school violence") you can integrate volunteering as a solution: "We should create a peer-mentoring program to reduce conflicts and in order to build a safer school environment."

✏️ Composition Practice: Volunteering Topic

📝 Exam-style prompt: persuasive paragraph

Prompt: “Some young people think volunteering is a waste of time. Write a short paragraph (about 100 words) to convince them that volunteering brings valuable benefits to both individuals and society. Use at least two purpose clauses.”
📌 Model answer:
“Volunteering is far from a waste of time. First, young people invest their free time to develop essential skills like leadership, communication, and problem-solving — skills that will help them in their future careers. Many students join local associations in order to gain real-world experience that school alone cannot provide. Moreover, communities rely on volunteers to improve public services, from health campaigns to environmental clean-ups. Volunteering also creates a sense of belonging and purpose. By giving back, you become an active citizen, not a passive observer. So next time you hesitate, remember: helping others is one of the smartest investments in your own future.”
🌟 Teacher’s note: Underline purpose structures in your exam. They are “signal words” that demonstrate grammatical range and clarity of argument.
