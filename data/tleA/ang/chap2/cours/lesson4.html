📌 Key concepts to remember

1️⃣ Essential job interview vocabulary

Knowing the right words helps you sound professional and confident. Use these terms during an interview or while preparing your application.

  • Interview : a formal meeting to assess your suitability for a job.
  • CV / résumé : a document listing your education, skills, and experience.
  • Qualifications : degrees, certificates or training that make you suitable for a position.
  • Experience : practical work or activities you have done in the past.
  • Skills : abilities you have developed (e.g., communication, teamwork, IT).
  • Strengths / Weaknesses : positive traits and areas for improvement.
  • Salary expectations : the amount of money you hope to earn.
  • Career goals : your long-term professional plans.

2️⃣ Grammar focus: Present perfect tense

Use the present perfect to talk about past experiences without a specific time. It's very common in interviews when you describe what you have done.

  • Structure: have/has + past participle
  • Examples:
    • “I have applied for several jobs.”
    • “She has worked as a volunteer in a hospital.”
    • “They have completed an internship in marketing.”
  • Key time expressions: already, yet, ever, never, just, recently, so far.
  • Why in interviews? It shows your achievements without focusing on when they happened. Example: “I have organised two school events” (sounds dynamic).

3️⃣ How to structure your answers (STAR method)

The STAR method helps you give complete and clear answers, especially for behavioural questions.

  • S – Situation: describe the context (where/when).
  • T – Task: explain the challenge or goal you had.
  • A – Action: detail the specific actions you took.
  • R – Result: share the outcome (success, learning, numbers if possible).

Example: “In my final year (Situation), I had to coordinate a class debate (Task). I organised teams and researched arguments (Action). The debate was a success and improved our public speaking skills (Result).”

💡 Comprehension examples

🗣️ Example 1 – Simulated interview dialogue

Context: You are applying for a part-time assistant position in a library. The interviewer asks about your experience with books and organisation.

Interviewer: “Tell me about your experience with organising events or materials.”
Candidate (you): “I have helped manage the school’s book club for two years. I have arranged weekly reading sessions and classified books by genre. Recently, I have also created a borrowing system that reduced lost books by 30%.”
Interviewer: “That’s impressive. Why do you want this job?”
Candidate: “I am passionate about literature and I believe my organisational skills will benefit the library team.”

✅ Why it works: The candidate uses present perfect (have helped, have arranged, have created) to highlight achievements. He/she connects personal passion with job requirements.

💬 Tip: Always give specific examples. Instead of saying “I am organised”, say “I have organised three school events with over 100 participants.”

📝 Example 2 – Using present perfect in interview answers

Rewrite the following sentences using the present perfect to make them more appropriate for a job interview. The first one is done for you.

  • Original: “I learn how to use Microsoft Office in 2022.” → Corrected: “I have learned how to use Microsoft Office.”
  • Original: “I finish a project on environmental awareness last month.” → Your turn: “I have finished a project on environmental awareness.”
  • Original: “She never works in a team before this year.” → Your turn: “She has never worked in a team before this year.”

Explanation: The present perfect connects past actions to the present moment. In an interview, it shows that your experiences are relevant now.

💡 Memory aid: “Have/Has + past participle” = experience without date. Avoid past simple when you want to highlight achievement.

✍️ Example 3 – Introducing yourself confidently

Prompt: Write two sentences to introduce yourself at the start of an interview for a teaching assistant role. Include your qualification and a relevant skill.

“Good morning. My name is Alice Ngo. I have obtained my Baccalaureate in Literature and I have volunteered as a tutor for younger students for one year. My communication skills and patience make me an excellent candidate for this position.”

Analysis: The introduction is polite, uses present perfect (have obtained, have volunteered), mentions a specific experience (tutoring) and links it to personal qualities. It is concise (2–3 sentences) but powerful.

⚠️ Common mistakes & how to avoid them

  • Mistake 1: Using past simple instead of present perfect to talk about general life experiences.
    ❌ “I did an internship last year.” (fine for a specific date but not highlighting relevance)
    ✅ “I have completed an internship, which taught me professional behaviour.” (better for interviews).
  • Mistake 2: Giving vague answers without examples.
    ❌ “I am a hard worker.”
    ✅ “I have consistently met deadlines for group projects, such as when we prepared the school magazine in two weeks.”
  • Mistake 3: Forgetting to use polite modals when asking questions.
    ❌ “Give me details about the salary.”
    ✅ “Could you please tell me more about the salary range?”
  • Mistake 4: Not preparing for common questions like “What are your weaknesses?”. Always show self-awareness and improvement.
    Example: “I sometimes struggle with public speaking, but I have joined the debate club to become more confident.”

🗣️ Bonus: key phrases to shine in an interview

Express yourself like a pro

  • Opening: “Thank you for having me. I am excited to be here.”
  • Talking about strengths: “One of my main strengths is ... / I have developed strong skills in ...”
  • Explaining past achievements: “I have successfully managed / organised / led ...”
  • Asking questions politely: “Could you describe a typical day in this role?” / “I would like to know more about the team.”
  • Closing: “Thank you for this opportunity. I look forward to hearing from you.”
💬 Pro tip: Use confident body language: maintain eye contact, sit upright, and smile. Non-verbal communication matters as much as words.

📖 Quick grammar table: present perfect

Use case Example (interview context)
Experience (ever/never) “Have you ever worked in customer service?” — “Yes, I have volunteered at a community centre.”
Recent past actions (just/already) “I have just finished an online course in digital marketing.”
Unfinished time (this year/so far) “So far this year, I have assisted in three school charity events.”

🎯 Remember: the present perfect always links the past to the present. It makes your experience sound current and relevant.
