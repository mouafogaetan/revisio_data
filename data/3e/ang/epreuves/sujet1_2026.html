Section A : Grammar – Tenses & Word Classes (16 points)

Exercise 1 : Simple Tenses (Present, Past, Future)

6 points

Complétez les phrases avec le temps verbal qui convient : Present Simple, Past Simple ou Future Simple (will).

  1. My sister usually ________ (to study) English for two hours every evening.
  2. Yesterday, the president ________ (to give) a speech about environmental protection.
  3. I think in the future, people ________ (to recycle) more plastic waste.
  4. They ________ (not / to finish) their homework last night because of the power cut.
  5. Water ________ (to boil) at 100 degrees Celsius. (fact)
  6. "I need some help!" — "Don't worry, I ________ (to help) you carry these books."

Exercise 2 : Continuous Tenses & 'going to'

5 points

Mettez les verbes entre parenthèses à la forme correcte (Present Continuous, Past Continuous, ou 'going to') :

  1. Listen! The birds ________ (sing) beautifully outside.
  2. While I ________ (prepare) dinner, my brother called me for an emergency.
  3. Look at those dark clouds! It ________ (rain) very soon.
  4. She can't answer the phone now because she ________ (take) a shower.
  5. We ________ (visit) our grandparents next weekend. We already bought the tickets.

5 points

Section C : Reading & Writing (12 points)

📄 Read the text carefully

Cameroon is often called "Africa in miniature" because of its diverse climates, cultures, and landscapes. In the northern region, droughts and desertification threaten local communities. Meanwhile, in coastal cities like Douala, poor waste management leads to flooding and waterborne diseases. To protect the environment, young Cameroonian activists encourage reforestation and the reduction of plastic bags. "We have only one planet," says Sandra, a 15-year-old student from Yaoundé. "If we act now, we can stop global warming." Governments also have a role: they must enforce laws against illegal logging. By working together, we can build a sustainable future.

A. Comprehension questions (4 points) :

  1. Why is Cameroon called "Africa in miniature" ?
  2. What environmental problem is mentioned in Douala ?
  3. According to the text, what do young activists encourage ?
  4. Find a synonym for "cutting trees illegally" in the text.

B. Vocabulary in context (2 points) : Explain the meaning of “droughts” and “reforestation”.

✍️ Writing Task (6 points)

Sujet :

“As a student in Cameroon, you are concerned about pollution in your neighborhood. Write a short paragraph (80-120 words) to describe the problem and propose two concrete solutions using the present simple, future simple (will) and at least two vocabulary words related to the environment (deforestation, waste, recycling, etc.).”
