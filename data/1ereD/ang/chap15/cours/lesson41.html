🌍 LESSON 41: Pollution and Environmental Issues

Anglais · Première CD · Révision Probatoire | Thème : Environnement

📌 Key Vocabulary & Core Concepts

🌱 What is pollution? (Qu'est-ce que la pollution ?)

Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances or products into the environment. These harmful substances are called pollutants. Pollution damages air, water, soil and affects human health, animals and plants.

Explication en français : La pollution, c'est l'introduction de substances nocives (déchets, produits chimiques, fumées) dans la nature, ce qui dégrade l'environnement et peut rendre les êtres vivants malades.


🎨 Illustration : sources de pollution (usine, voitures, déchets)
  • Pollutant = a substance that pollutes (ex: smoke, plastic, chemical waste).
  • Environment = the natural world (land, water, air, living things).
  • Harmful = causing damage or injury.

🚫 Main types of pollution (Principaux types de pollution)

Voici les quatre grandes catégories à connaître pour l'examen. Chaque type a ses causes et conséquences spécifiques.

Air pollution Water pollution Soil pollution Noise pollution
  • 🌫️ Air pollution : caused by smoke from factories, vehicle exhaust, burning of waste.
    → Conséquences : respiratory diseases, global warming, acid rain.
  • 💧 Water pollution : caused by industrial waste, oil spills, plastic dumping, pesticides.
    → Conséquences : kills aquatic life, contaminated drinking water.
  • 🌾 Soil pollution : caused by chemicals, pesticides, landfill waste, nuclear waste.
    → Conséquences : reduced soil fertility, crops contaminated, harm to animals.
  • 🔊 Noise pollution : caused by traffic, construction, loud music, factories.
    → Conséquences : hearing loss, stress, sleep disorders.
💡 Astuce pour mémoriser : "A.W.S.N" → Air, Water, Soil, Noise. Pensez aux quatre ennemis de la planète !

🔥 Causes & Consequences (Causes et conséquences majeures)

Les activités humaines sont responsables de la majorité de la pollution. Il est essentiel de maîtriser ce vocabulaire pour décrire les problèmes environnementaux.

  • Deforestation (déforestation) : cutting down trees → less oxygen, more CO₂, habitat destruction.
  • Industrialization (industrialisation) : factories release toxic gases and chemical waste.
  • Overuse of plastic (surexploitation du plastique) : plastic waste in oceans → danger for marine animals.
  • Global warming (réchauffement climatique) : increase in Earth's temperature due to greenhouse gases (CO₂, methane).
  • Acid rain (pluies acides) : caused by sulfur dioxide from factories → damages forests and buildings.

💡 Examples in context (compréhension & production)

🌬️ Example 1 – Describing air pollution

Situation : In Douala, many cars and factories release black smoke. A journalist is describing the situation.

✅ Correct sentence : “The air pollution in Douala is getting worse because of vehicle exhaust and industrial emissions. This causes respiratory diseases among the population.”

💬 Explication française : On utilise le présent simple (“is getting”, “causes”) pour décrire une réalité générale. “Vehicle exhaust” = gaz d'échappement ; “respiratory diseases” = maladies respiratoires.

♻️ Example 2 – Expressing consequences and solutions

Question type (exam) : “What are the effects of water pollution on communities?”

Sample answer : “Water pollution leads to the death of fish and other aquatic animals. Moreover, people who drink contaminated water can suffer from diseases like cholera. To reduce water pollution, factories must treat their waste before releasing it into rivers.”

  • Key linking words : “leads to”, “moreover”, “to reduce”.
  • Vocabulary : contaminated water (eau contaminée), treat waste (traiter les déchets).

🔊 Example 3 – Noise pollution in neighbourhoods

Two students are talking about the constant noise from a nearby bar.

A: “I can't concentrate on my homework because of the loud music. It's a serious case of noise pollution.”
B: “You're right. Noise pollution can cause stress and sleeping problems. We should report it to the local authorities.”

📘 Astuce grammaire : “can cause” (peut causer) exprime une possibilité / conséquence typique. “should report” (devrions signaler) = conseil.

⚠️ Common mistakes & false friends

  • ❌ Erreur fréquente : confondre "waste" (déchets) et "wastage" (gaspillage). Pollution vient souvent de industrial waste (déchets industriels).
  • 🔍 Attention : "litter" = déchets jetés par terre (canettes, papiers) ; "littering" est une action interdite. Ne pas confondre avec "litter" (portée d'animaux).
  • ⚠️ À ne pas confondre : "toxic" (toxique) et "poisonous" (venimeux/toxique pour ingestion). En environnement, on dit souvent "toxic chemicals".
  • 📝 Faux-ami : "eventually" ne veut pas dire "éventuellement" mais "finalement". Exemple : "Pollution will eventually destroy ecosystems." (La pollution finira par détruire les écosystèmes).
💡 Pour le probatoire : utilisez toujours des adjectifs précis (airborne, waterborne, chemical) devant “pollution” pour montrer votre maîtrise du lexique.

📚 Useful phrases to express environmental issues

🗣️ For written or oral production (Probatoire)

  • To be responsible for : “Human activities are responsible for air pollution.”
  • To pose a threat to : “Plastic waste poses a serious threat to marine life.”
  • To have a devastating impact on : “Oil spills have a devastating impact on coastal ecosystems.”
  • To raise awareness about : “NGOs raise awareness about the dangers of soil pollution.”
  • To tackle pollution : “Governments must take measures to tackle pollution.”

✍️ Exemple d'ouverture de paragraphe : “Nowadays, environmental issues such as air and water pollution have become a major concern in Cameroon. In fact, rapid urbanization and industrial waste are increasing day by day.”
