📖 Lesson 12: Reported Statements

English · Première CD · Discours Rapporté (Statements)

🎯 Objectifs de la leçon

  • Transformer des déclarations directes en discours indirect (reported speech).
  • Maîtriser le changement des temps (tense backshift) : present → past, past → past perfect.
  • Adapter correctement les pronoms personnels, adjectifs possessifs et les indicateurs de temps/lieu.
  • Comprendre la différence entre "say" et "tell" + construction.

📌 Règles fondamentales

1. Le « backshift » (recul des temps)

Quand le verbe introducteur est au passé (said, told, asked, etc.), le temps du verbe dans la phrase rapportée recule d’un cran dans le passé.

Temps dans le discours directTemps dans le discours indirectExemple direct → indirect
Present Simple (he works)Past Simple (he worked)"I love pizza." → He said he loved pizza.
Present Continuous (is cooking)Past Continuous (was cooking)"We are studying" → They said they were studying.
Past Simple (I went)Past Perfect (had gone)"She arrived late." → He said she had arrived late.
Present Perfect (has eaten)Past Perfect (had eaten)"I have seen that film." → He said he had seen that film.
will / can / maywould / could / might"I will help" → He said he would help.
💡 Astuce : Si le verbe introducteur est au présent (says, tells), le temps ne change PAS.
Exemple : "Paul says: 'I am tired'." → Paul says he is tired.

2. Changement des pronoms, adjectifs possessifs et références

Il faut adapter les pronoms (I → he/she, we → they, my → his/her, our → their) selon le sujet du discours rapporté.

  • Direct : "I my book" → Indirect : He said his book.
  • Direct : "We our project" → They said their project.
  • Attention : "you" peut devenir "he/she/they" selon le contexte.

3. Indicateurs de temps et de lieu

Les mots qui indiquent le moment ou l’endroit changent souvent pour refléter la perspective du narrateur.

Discours directDiscours indirectExemple
nowthen"I am busy now" → He said he was busy then.
todaythat day"She works today" → She said she worked that day.
tomorrowthe next day / the following day"We will arrive tomorrow" → They said they would arrive the next day.
yesterdaythe day before / the previous day"I called yesterday" → He said he had called the day before.
herethere"Come here" → He told me to go there.
this / thesethat / those"I like this car" → He said he liked that car.

🔁 SAY vs TELL : différence essentielle

En anglais, on utilise say ou tell pour introduire le discours rapporté, mais la construction est différente.

  • Say + (that) + proposition → On ne précise PAS à qui on parle.
    Ex: "He said (that) he was happy."
  • Tell + complément (personne) + (that) + proposition → On précise la personne à qui l’on parle.
    Ex: "He told me (that) he was happy."
⚠️ On ne dit pas "He told that he was happy" (il manque la personne).
✅ On dit : "He told me/her/us that..."

💡 Exemples de compréhension (avec explications en français)

Exemple 1 : Changement de temps (Present Simple → Past Simple)

🗣️ Discours direct : John said: "I work hard every day."

Rapporté : John said (that) he worked hard every day.
📝 Explication : Le verbe introducteur "said" étant au passé, le present simple "work" devient past simple "worked". Le pronom "I" devient "he" (John est un homme).

💬 Astuce : En français, on dirait « John a dit qu'il travaillait dur chaque jour ». Le même recul du temps existe !

Exemple 2 : Present Perfect → Past Perfect

🗣️ Discours direct : Mary said: "I have already eaten lunch."

Rapporté : Mary said (that) she had already eaten lunch.
📝 Explication : "have eaten" (present perfect) devient "had eaten" (past perfect) après "said". Le mot "already" reste identique.

Exemple 3 : Changement de pronoms et adverbe de lieu

🗣️ Discours direct : Peter told us: "I will meet you here tomorrow."

Rapporté : Peter told us (that) he would meet us there the next day.
📝 Explications :

  • "will meet" → "would meet" (will → would).
  • "you" (désignant les auditeurs) → "us" car Peter s'adressait à "nous".
  • "here" → "there" (changement de lieu).
  • "tomorrow" → "the next day".

Exemple 4 : Past Simple → Past Perfect (action antérieure)

🗣️ Discours direct : She said: "I bought a new phone yesterday."

Rapporté : She said (that) she had bought a new phone the day before.
📝 Explication : "bought" (past simple) → "had bought" (past perfect). "yesterday" devient "the day before".

Exemple 5 : Utilisation de "that" (facultatif)

🗣️ Discours direct : The teacher said: "English is important."

Rapporté : The teacher said that English was important.
📝 Explication : On peut garder "that" ou l'omettre. Le verbe "is" devient "was".
✅ The teacher said English was important. (correct aussi)

⚠️ Pièges courants & erreurs à éviter

  • Oublier le backshift : Dire *"He said he is tired" alors que le contexte est passé → ❌ Il faut "He said he was tired".
  • Confondre say et tell : *"She told that she was happy" → ❌ Oubli de la personne. Corriger : "She told me that..." ou "She said that...".
  • Ne pas changer les pronoms : "I said: 'I like my car'" → Ne pas rapporter "He said I liked my car" si le locuteur est différent. → ✅ "He said he liked his car".
  • Indicateurs de temps figés : Si on rapporte le jour même, "today" peut rester "today" selon le contexte. Mais en général, l’exige du bac, on applique le changement systématique.
  • Verbe introducteur au présent : Si la phrase commence par "He says...", il ne faut PAS changer les temps. Exemple : He says "I am happy" → He says he is happy (pas "was").
